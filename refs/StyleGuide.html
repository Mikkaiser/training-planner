01

Principles

The decisions that shaped every component. Apply these before reaching for tokens.

01

Inline, never away.

Every action — adding phases, blocks, exercises, marking gates — happens in place. The only redirect is between list and detail. The only modal is plan creation.

02

Earned accent.

Lime is for what's now — current phase, active block, primary CTA. If everything is highlighted, nothing is. Restraint makes it sing.

03

Warm, not white.

The page background is a soft off-white with a lime radial bleed from the top-right. Premium and warm; never sterile or flat.

04

Mono carries data.

JetBrains Mono signals precision: verb levels, file types, gate codes, phase indices, percentages. Sans carries voice; mono carries metric.

05

Structure beats ornament.

Typographic hierarchy, generous space, and a strict 2-color palette do the heavy lifting. No gradient buttons, no decorative icons.

06

Cumulative truth.

Gates are auto-generated, never invented. Status pills are emphatic — passed, pending, failed — because they describe earned mastery, not opinion.

Voice in one paragraph

You are the instructor's quiet partner. Speak like a coach taking notes on a clipboard — direct, useful, never cheerleading. Numbers are facts. Verb levels are calibrated. A failed gate isn't a setback; it's remediation — one specific block, named clearly, ready to retry.

09

Voice in copy

A short do/don't list. The tone is a calm coach with a clipboard.

Do
  • "Cumulative — Blocks 1 through 7"
  • "Mark Passed" / "Mark Failed"
  • "3/5 blocks · Gate 4 pending"
  • "Start by adding your first phase."
  • "A blank roadmap, a clear horizon."
Don't
  • "Awesome! 🎉 You crushed it!"
  • "Pass this assessment to continue"
  • "60% of blocks have been completed by you"
  • "Get started by clicking the button below"
  • "Don't worry if you fail — that's totally okay!"
